Budget By School

JSOM Expenses

JSOM: JSOM is UT Dallas’ primary business school, generating $13.0M in designated tuition. The school spends $78.5M, with most funding concentrated in management programs and teaching salaries.

NSM Expenses

NSM: NSM supports core science instruction and lab-based research with $38.3M in designated expenses. Funding is split between teaching salaries and research costs, with lab and equipment needs limiting budget flexibility.

AHT Expenses

AHT Expenses: According to figure 20, 98.7% of expenses are dedicated to salaries and other wages. There is approximately a $3.8M gap in net funding when comparing designated tuition to expenses.

BBS Expenses

BBS – Expenses: According to Figure 23, 47% of expenses are dedicated to salaries and other wages. BBS shows a structural reliance on external funding from grants and University allocations.

EPPS Expenses

EPPS – Expenses: According to Figure 25, 57.3% of expenses are dedicated to salaries and other wages. EPPS shows a structural reliance on institutional allocations and external funding.

IS Expenses

IS – Expenses: IS supports interdisciplinary academic programs and operates on a $2.6M designated budget, the smallest among the schools analyzed. Most spending goes to salaries, making the school lean but sensitive to budget changes.

AHT Revenue

AHT - Revenue: The total revenue from the School of AHT is $9.2M with Arts Humanities & Technology. The revenue generated is highly concentrated to AHT which creates a unique structure for AHT with less reliance on institution allocations.
